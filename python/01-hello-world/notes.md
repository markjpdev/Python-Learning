## Initial experience coming from someone who used to code 15 years ago in C, C++, VB 6.0, and VB.NET

Python is straightforward once you get the hang of it. But coming from languages where you had to declare variables, import libraries, and follow strict structure before writing a single line of logic — Python feels almost too simple at first.

One liner. No boilerplate. It reads like human language.

I kept waiting for the imports, the declarations, the setup. In Python, for basic scripts, you just write. That informality threw me off initially but I can already see why it works.

No intellisense required at this level either — which is another adjustment. In VB.NET and C++ I relied on it heavily. Here it feels unnecessary, at least for now.

15 years of structured languages leave a mark. But the simplicity is growing on me.
